## NJ Licensing Requirements

### School-Level Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Licensing authority | NJ Motor Vehicle Commission (NJMVC) Business Licensing Services (BLS) |
| Surety bond | $10,000, issued by NJ-authorized surety; required for license issuance and renewal |
| License fee | $250 application fee (statute) |
| Qualified supervising instructor | Required on staff; must be (a) licensed instructor for 2+ years, (b) 500+ hrs BTW instruction, (c) completed a 3-credit NJ driver education college course |
| Fingerprints + background checks | Required for owner/applicant and instructors |
| Office accessibility | Must be accessible during regular business hours; can use answering machine if unstaffed |

**Critical dependency:** If neither Matt nor existing staff qualifies as Supervising Instructor at closing, there is no path to license. Must verify this on the existing operator before any offer.

### Instructor Licensing

- Minimum age: 21
- NJ license held for 3+ consecutive years
- Criminal history check (NJ State Police, N.J.A.C. 13:59)
- Renewal must be filed at least 30 days before expiration
- Instructors hired after a certain date require NSC defensive driving course completion

### Vehicle Compliance

- Dual controls on foot brake (and clutch if applicable) — required
- Seat belts for student and instructor — required
- Inside and outside mirrors for both — required
- Insurance minimums: $250K/$500K/$50K split limits OR $500K CSL
- Certificate of insurance listing each vehicle, with 30-day cancellation notice to NJMVC
- NJMVC can inspect vehicles with 24 hours notice during business hours
- Signage: roof sign (optional) with business name + license number; if no roof sign, two exterior door signs required

### Student Instruction Constraints

- Max 3 hours of BTW instruction per student per calendar day
- Scheduling implication: cannot stack two 2-hour lessons for same student same day
- Under-21 permit holders (post Feb 2025): 50 total supervised hours required (10 at night) before probationary license
- 72-hour rescission right after first lesson (service agreement required)

### Recordkeeping

- Service agreements: mandatory, must state services and fees
- Student records must include: name, DL number, instructor name, date/type/duration of services
- Instructor records: identity, license number, employment dates, BTW hours delivered
- Retention: 3 years at principal place of business; subject to audit
- Electronic storage is permitted

## Local Municipal Requirements (Manalapan / Monmouth County)

- **Zoning approval required** before commercial Certificate of Continued Occupancy (CCO) — do not sign a lease without zoning confirmation first
- CCO fees and sign permit fees apply per township construction code schedule
- Fire Prevention business registration may be required after occupancy
- Trade name filing: if operating as sole proprietor/GP under a DBA, file Certificate of Trade Name with Monmouth County Clerk

**Key contact:** Manalapan Zoning Dept — request zoning determination in writing before signing a lease.

## Acquisition Due Diligence Checklist (Regulatory)

| Area | Documents to Get | Red Flags |
|---|---|---|
| NJMVC license status | Current school license, BLS correspondence, proof of supervising instructor quals | Any lapse, pending enforcement, inability to document supervising instructor |
| Instructor roster | Licenses, renewal dates, background check submissions, driving abstracts | Unlicensed instruction; renewals not filed 30 days prior |
| Insurance + bond | Certs of insurance per vehicle, cancellation notices, bond docs + premium history | Coverage gaps, noncompliant limits, cancellation notices |
| Vehicles | Titles/leases, dual control invoices, maintenance logs, signage compliance | Missing dual controls; no maintenance records; vehicle not on insurance |
| Student contracts + records | Service agreements, student record samples, retention practices | No service agreements; records below 3-yr standard; records not at principal location |
| Financials | Tax returns, P&L, bank statements, deferred revenue (prepaid packages), chargebacks | Revenue recognition issues; large outstanding lesson obligations |
| Legal exposure | Claims history, accident logs, demand letters, settlements, complaint complaints | High claims frequency; unreported incidents |
| Municipal compliance | Lease, zoning approval, CCO docs, sign permits | Location not properly zoned; CCO not transferable to buyer |

## Key Compliance Risks for This Acquisition

1. **Supervising Instructor continuity** — the single most critical compliance risk; the license cannot be renewed without one on staff. If the current owner IS the qualifying supervising instructor and leaves, the license is at risk. Must be the first diligence question.

2. **Deferred revenue / prepaid lesson obligations** — road tests booked a year out and lessons booked 6 weeks out suggest significant prepaid balances. These are liabilities the buyer inherits. Must get a deferred revenue schedule.

3. **Independent contractor vs. employee** — NJ uses the ABC test; instructors are likely employees. If any have been misclassified, there may be back liability for taxes, workers comp, and benefits.

4. **CCO and lease assignability** — buying the business without buying the property means inheriting leases. Marlboro DS lease expires Mar 2027. Confirm: are leases assignable to a buyer? Is there a landlord consent requirement?

5. **Vehicle insurance continuity** — certificates must list each vehicle with 30-day cancellation notice. During a transition, must ensure no coverage gap.

## Staffing and Hiring Notes

- Instructor hiring timeline: not fast; requires age/license checks, background check, DMV written + driving test
- Supervising Instructor: 500 hrs BTW instruction (achievable in ~3 months at current volume) + 2 years OR 3-credit college course
- NJ average instructor wage: ~$24.61/hr (benchmark)
- Misclassification risk real in NJ; build model assuming W-2 (already the case at this business)
