# April 2026 Search Advertising Performance Summary
**For:** Einhorn Barbarito  
**Period:** April 1-30, 2026  
**Campaign Focus:** Google Search - Employment Law & General Legal Services

---

## The Bottom Line

April was a strong month. We nearly **tripled lead volume** from March while actually reducing what each lead costs us.

- **209 qualified leads** (139 form submissions + 70 phone calls)
- **$24 per lead** (down from $34 in March)
- **$5,021 total spend**

---

## What Changed Month-over-Month (March vs. April)

| Metric | March | April | Change |
|--------|-------|-------|--------|
| Total Leads | 74 | 209 | +181% |
| Cost Per Lead | $34 | $24 | -30% |
| Budget | $2,551 | $5,021 | +97% |
| Lead Quality (conversion rate) | 3.6% | 13.0% | +263% |

**What this means:** When we doubled the budget, we got three times the leads at a lower cost. That's the inverse of what normally happens as you scale, which suggests our targeting is hitting the right audience.

---

## Campaign Performance Breakdown

**Employment Law Campaign** (the winner)
- 140 leads at $21.45 each
- This was our most efficient campaign. Every dollar spent here delivered the best results.

**General Legal Services Campaign**
- 69 leads at $29.03 each
- Still solid, but about 35% more expensive than Employment Law. This is where we're seeing room to optimize.

**Competitor Keywords Campaign**
- Minimal spend ($0.44), minimal return
- Not worth focusing on right now.

---

## What's Driving Results

**Top-Performing Searches:**

1. "Employment Lawyer" — 79 leads
   - This single keyword accounted for 38% of our leads. Strong intent, good quality.

2. "Lawyers Near Me" — 16 leads
   - Classic intent-driven search. People actively looking for local counsel.

3. "Law Office Near Me" — 14 leads
   - Same intent as above. Geographic targeting is working.

**Device Performance:**
- 76% of clicks come from mobile phones
- This is critical: your landing page experience on mobile directly impacts conversion rates. Any friction there kills lead volume.

---

## What's Working, What Isn't

**Working:**
- Employment law keywords are your profit center. Very efficient customer acquisition.
- Leads-focused campaign creative and messaging is resonating.
- Mobile traffic volume is strong and converting well.

**Not Working:**
- "Brand" keyword searches ("best divorce lawyers," "top divorce lawyers") generate zero leads.
- Property law and family law keywords underperform relative to employment law.
- Competitor conquest terms don't move the needle.

---

## What's Next (May Planning)

We're recommending three focused moves:

1. **Double down on Employment Law** — This is your most efficient channel. Increase budget allocation here.

2. **Launch Personal Injury Campaign** — Early testing suggests strong intent and volume in this vertical. Worth a dedicated test budget.

3. **Improve Mobile Experience** — 3 out of 4 clicks come from phones. Any improvements to form speed or mobile landing page design will directly increase conversions.

---

## Questions?

The data is solid. The question now is how fast you want to scale. Budget doubled and leads tripled. We can push further, but want to make sure operations (follow-up, intake, etc.) can handle the volume.
