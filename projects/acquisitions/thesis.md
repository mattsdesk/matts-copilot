# Acquisition Thesis
**Version:** v0.9
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-21

## Strategy: Barbell

Two distinct tracks, evaluated against different criteria. The digest surfaces both, labeled clearly.

- **Primary Operating Business.** One anchor acquisition. Hands-on leadership role. $250K to $1M SDE.
- **Passive Holds.** Secondary portfolio additions. Only categories where passive operation is structural, not aspirational. $100K to $300K SDE.

The Primary track is where Matt spends his time. Passive Holds are bolt-ons acquired opportunistically once the Primary is running.

The thesis is the filter. Discovery, triage, and vet all flow from this document. Update it as Matt's view sharpens.

- **Multiple ceiling:** 3.5x. Willing to stretch for exceptional quality and clear growth path.

**Passive Holds:**

- **Multiple ceiling:** 3.0x. Passive holds are bought on cash yield and asset quality; no stretch on multiple.

## Involvement: Work ON, not IN (applies to Primary track)

This is a primary filter for the Primary Operating Business track. Matt wants to work ON the business (owner/CEO role: strategy, growth, systems, hiring, capital allocation) not IN it (delivering the day-to-day service, being the lead technician, covering the owner's operational shifts). The goal is to buy a business, not a job.

**Required for a strong match:**
- Owner's current role is already primarily leadership and oversight, or there is a clear path to that within 6 to 12 months
- Staff or management layer exists who deliver the revenue. Matt is not the last line of service delivery.
- Revenue and customer relationships are transferable without the owner or Matt personally executing the work
- Systems, SOPs, and institutional knowledge live outside the owner's head

**Yellow flag (not automatic kill, but raises the bar):**
- Owner works 40+ hours/week in frontline operations
- Business would stop producing if the owner disappeared for a month
- "The owner IS the brand" dynamics (personal brand businesses, solo professional practices)

**Hard kill:**
- Solo operator with no staff and no realistic path to hiring out the operator role
- Licensed-trade business where the owner personally holds the one license that authorizes the work and the license is not easily replicated

This filter is about the role Matt plays post-close, not about whether he works hard. He is fine working hard. He is not fine being the person who has to show up to do the work every day for the business to make money.

## Passive Holds: Structural Passivity Test

For the Passive Holds track, the operating-involvement filter is replaced by this structural test. A Passive Hold qualifies only if the business model itself does not require active owner attention.

**Must pass all of:**
- Revenue is generated by an asset (real estate, equipment, software, license), a route with existing operators, or a team of contractors/employees already in place
- Customer acquisition is passive, automated, or systemic (e.g., walk-up foot traffic, recurring subscriptions, contracted routes), not driven by owner relationships
- Day-to-day operations are either self-service (storage, car wash, SaaS) or delegated to an existing operator
- Owner's current time commitment is verifiable at 5 to 10 hours/week or less, and that number is defensible on inspection

**Hard kill for Passive Holds:**
- "Absentee" service businesses where the owner claims 15 hours/week but is still the relationship holder or crisis responder (most "absentee" service listings fail this)
- Any business where replacing the owner would require hiring a skilled operator from scratch
- Food businesses (already excluded globally)
- Businesses where passive operation depends on a single key employee

Think "real estate with extra steps" as the mental model for Passive Holds.

## Sectors

**Primary Operating Business, in scope:**
- Services (B2B or B2C)
- Education and training (driving schools, music schools, tutoring, trades training)
- Physical product, distribution, light manufacturing
- Brick and mortar retail (case by case)
- Digital / online businesses: open but lower priority. Concern: durability of digital moats in the age of AI (content sites, SEO-dependent businesses, middleware, commoditized SaaS). Prefer digital businesses with durable non-AI moats (e.g., network effects, physical-world data, regulated niches, strong brand, long-term customer contracts). Treat pure content, thin SaaS, and SEO-dependent businesses with extra skepticism on defensibility.

**Passive Holds, in scope (narrow list):**
- Self-storage and mini-storage facilities
- Automated / express car washes (not full-service)
- Vending routes, ATM routes, amusement/game routes (with route operator in place)
- Equipment rental (party, construction tool, medical, etc.)
- Campgrounds, RV parks, marinas (real-estate anchored)
- Digital businesses with structural passivity: established SaaS on autopilot, directory sites, affiliate sites with stable organic traffic (with AI-durability test applied)
- Licensing or royalty revenue businesses

Passive Holds must pass a "structurally passive" test: the business model itself does not require active owner attention, not "the owner says they only work 15 hours a week."

**Hard exclude (sectors):**
- Food businesses (restaurants, food service, bakeries, cafes, bars, catering, food production)

**Hard exclude (specific business types):**
- Bread routes and route-based distribution (low margin, logistics-heavy, hard to scale)
- Laundromats (commoditized, equipment-heavy, real-estate-dependent)

Approach: open minus the above. This exclude list will grow as Matt ID's more business types he doesn't want. Add new exclusions here as they come up.

## Business Quality Filters

Starting heuristics. Refine based on deals that come through.

- Minimum 5 years in operation
- Recurring, contractual, or repeat revenue preferred over purely transactional
- Owner not the sole revenue generator (customer relationships must be transferable)
- Documented financials: tax returns, not just seller P&L
- No single customer above ~25% of revenue
- Staff in place (not a solopreneur operation unless the role is genuinely replaceable)

## Deal Structure Preferences

- **Seller financing:** strongly preferred. Strong signal of seller confidence.
- **SBA 7a:** acceptable
- **Asset sale vs stock sale:** favor asset sale for liability protection unless tax or regulatory structure forces stock
- **Seller transition:** 6 to 12 months preferred for knowledge transfer
- **Earnouts:** open, especially if gating on revenue continuity

## Red Flags (not automatic kills, but raise scrutiny)

- Single key employee or license holder
- Declining revenue or margins over past 3 years
- Heavy regulatory exposure with owner-held credentials
- Thin or inconsistent financial documentation
- Vague "reason for sale"
- Owner's actual role materially greater than disclosed
