## TouchTunes Company Overview

TouchTunes is a social entertainment platform expanding beyond music jukeboxes. The app has a 4.9-star rating with strong user engagement. Whale customers behave like subscription users, averaging $10 per month. They recently acquired a darts company to expand their platform. The company is private equity owned and seeking growth for a future exit after being held as an asset for 4+ years.

## Current Product and Technology Challenges

The tech stack spans three programming languages across four apps: native iOS and Android for music, Flutter for the acquired darts business, and they're evaluating React Native consolidation for a modular architecture.

Infrastructure modernization is needed. They're running 2014-era systems versus modern Kubernetes, with manual scaling instead of auto-scaling. However, they've achieved a 40% improvement in deployment frequency.

Team perfectionism is slowing velocity. An 8-week project took over 4 months, and they over-analyze during percentage rollouts (1% to 100%).

## Strategic Growth Initiatives for 2026

Next-gen jukebox hardware includes a 43-inch screen (mega iPhone-style) that enables advertising integration and bigger UI for new adjacencies.

2026 priorities are: (1) social features within the app, (2) advertising integration with revenue sharing for operators, and (3) payment solutions for non-connected devices. The vision is TouchTunes as a consumer entertainment gateway, not just a music brand.

## Team Development Needs

Product managers are skilled but need strategic elevation. They're strong on UX details and feature execution but have gaps in business strategy and GM-level thinking. They need coaching on revenue impact, churn rates, and strategic trade-offs.

The Montreal-based engineering team operates with hybrid culture and travel every other week is required. They recently completed a difficult team restructuring, and cultural nuances are important for leadership success.

## Market Context and Challenges

The venue landscape is shifting. Traditional bars and restaurants are slowing or closing, and Gen Z prefers coffee shops and laundromats over bars. They're testing Android tablet jukeboxes for new venues.

Competition for attention in the social entertainment space is fierce. The operator community has a strong voice in product decisions, with the revenue sharing model strengthening relationships and encouraging a strategic partnership approach rather than transactional.
