## TouchTunes Business Overview

Hardware + software platform for jukeboxes and dartboards in 160,000 locations. Revenue model: recurring payments from consumers playing songs ($), not hardware sales. 3.5M monthly active users on mobile app.

Geographic distribution:
- Montreal: original HQ, technology development (French Canadian workforce)
- Schaumburg, Illinois: sales and operations hub
- New York: music/legal/finance/marketing teams + CEO Scott
- UK: small team

90% sales through operator channel, 10% strategic accounts. Operators own location relationships, TouchTunes provides technology. Saturated bar/restaurant market, need expansion to new venue types.

---

## Product Complexity and Challenges

Multi-stakeholder dynamics create complexity:
- Consumers (song players) want features/low prices
- Operators want profitability/reliability
- Distributors in operator channel

Hardware constraints: "once it's out the door, it's out the door" vs software iteration.

Social features expansion requires safety considerations. Example: user posts song in location, others can like song but safety needed to prevent unwanted personal contact.

Dartboard opportunity: 48,000 units deployed but no recurring revenue model yet.

New jukebox design in progress with Malaysian contract manufacturer (Flextronics). Dartboards manufactured in-house 1 hour west of Schaumburg.

---

## Organizational Dynamics and Success Factors

PMO reporting to Dan, focused on delivery while product drives discovery. Customer service director acts as "Voice of Customer" leader. Weekly intake review process balances short-term revenue wins vs long-term impact.

Key success differentiators:
1. Planning rigor
2. Constant communication
3. Maintaining focus/vigor

Main failure mode: too many priorities spreading key players too thin. Current challenge: 6 hot initiatives running simultaneously. Product managers juggling 2-3 priorities each.

Heavy A/B testing and data-driven decision making. Weekly dashboards track technician/operator feedback.
