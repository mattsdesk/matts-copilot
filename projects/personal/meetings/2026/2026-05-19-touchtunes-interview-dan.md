## TouchTunes Business Overview

TouchTunes is a hardware and software platform for jukeboxes and dartboards operating in 160,000 locations. Their revenue model is based on recurring payments from consumers playing songs, not hardware sales. They have 3.5M monthly active users on their mobile app.

Geographic distribution spans Montreal (original HQ, technology development with French Canadian workforce), Schaumburg Illinois (sales and operations hub), New York (music, legal, finance, marketing teams plus CEO Scott), and a small UK team.

90% of sales come through the operator channel (location owners who provide venue relationships), with 10% from strategic accounts. The bar and restaurant market is saturated, so they're seeking expansion into new venue types.

## Product Complexity and Challenges

Multi-stakeholder dynamics create complexity across consumers (who want features and low prices), operators (who want profitability and reliability), and distributors in the operator channel.

Hardware constraints are significant: once a unit ships, it's out in the field, whereas software can iterate. Social features expansion requires careful safety considerations. For example, when users post songs in a location that others can like, the system must prevent unwanted personal contact.

The dartboard opportunity includes 48,000 units deployed but no recurring revenue model yet. A new jukebox design is in progress with Malaysian contract manufacturer Flextronics, while dartboards are manufactured in-house 1 hour west of Schaumburg.

## Organizational Dynamics and Success Factors

The PMO reports to Dan and focuses on delivery while product drives discovery. The customer service director acts as "Voice of Customer" leader. A weekly intake review process balances short-term revenue wins against long-term impact.

Key success differentiators are planning rigor, constant communication, and maintaining focus and vigor. The main failure mode is too many priorities spreading key players too thin. Currently, 6 hot initiatives are running simultaneously with product managers juggling 2-3 priorities each.

The organization uses heavy A/B testing and data-driven decision making, with weekly dashboards tracking technician and operator feedback.
