## Interview Overview

Anand Natrajan, Engineering Leader at Warner Bros Discovery. Role focus: CMS system for Bleacher Report. Connection through Sean Riley (Disney).

## Bleacher Report User Experience & Content Strategy

Matt's usage patterns: home screen scores for spoiler-free updates, feed personalization based on selected teams, values behind-the-scenes content that deepens fandom.

Key insight: Fans read about rivals as much as their own teams. Emotional connection drives engagement beyond stated preferences. Example: Michigan fans consuming Ohio State content.

Fandom components:
1. Emotionally resonant storytelling
2. Sense of belonging/insider connection

## Content Management & Personalization Balance

Four competing forces in CMS:
1. Revenue generation
2. Explicit user preferences (followed teams)
3. Implicit preferences (rivalry engagement)
4. Serendipitous discovery (new fandoms)

Matt's Fubo approach:
- Primary metric: minutes watched to retention correlation
- Lookalike audience selection most effective for recommendations
- Balance subscription content vs FAST channels (higher ad revenue)
- Constant A/B testing on critical carousels (90% of selections in 3 carousels)

## Data Analysis & Decision Making

Leadership philosophy: "Leading from the back" empowering team with trust and ownership.

Sets North Star metrics, guides execution.

Three-pronged approach to data vs domain expertise:
1. Empowered, engaged teams (not "Excel jockeys")
2. Qualitative research for "why" behind data patterns
3. Collective decades of experience for judgment calls

Statistical significance requires understanding root causes.

Example: Personalization impact (using names) vs minor wording changes.

## Platform Architecture & Stakeholder Management

Anand's Bleacher Report 2.0 experience:
- Successful culture improvements: engineering-product collaboration
- Regretted cuts: social features (following users, notifications)
- Video-forward strategy backfired with lost social engagement

Current challenges:
- Business-engineering relationship still needs work
- Over-reliance on manual content curation vs technology
- Personalization framework needs improvement

## Growth Opportunities & Technical Priorities

Biggest opportunity: Rapid sport onboarding capability. Currently 6-12 months to add new sports. Target: <1 quarter for new sport integration.

Personalization improvements: reduce manual editorial decisions, better rivalry/emotional connection algorithms, more nuanced user preference modeling.

Trust-building through "shared pain" in-person collaboration essential.
