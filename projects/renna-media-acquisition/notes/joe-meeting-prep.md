# Joe Renna Meeting Prep

**Date:** Thursday 2026-04-30, ~12:00 PM
**Location:** Diner in Cranford NJ (Joe's regular spot)
**Attendees:** Joe Renna, Eric Sharret (broker), Matt Saunders
**Format:** Lunch, informal, ~90 minutes

---

## Mission

This is a chemistry meeting first, diligence meeting second. Joe is choosing a buyer he believes in. Three buyers have died in the last 12 months because Joe rejected them as lacking growth vision and operating chops. Win the rapport, surface enough operating signal to advance, do not torch the relationship by anchoring on price.

The deliverable from this meeting is Joe's verbal blessing to move toward an LOI, plus access to the next layer of financial documentation.

---

## What Joe Needs to See in You

Per Eric, this is what killed the last deal and what Joe is screening for:

1. **You are an operator.** You have run businesses, formed companies, hired and managed.
2. **You have a growth thesis.** Not "I'll figure it out." A specific point of view.
3. **You will hire and not try to do everything yourself.** You see this as a CEO seat, not a self-employment seat.
4. **You respect his legacy.** This is the work of his life.
5. **You can carry the seller note.** He is putting half a million on the line for you.

## What to Ask Joe (in priority order)

Build rapport first. Ask about origins, the towns, his favorite stories, his proudest issue. Then move into operating questions. Save anything that smells like negotiation for after the relationship is set.

### Origin and pride
- How did Kenilworth, the first paper, come together
- Which town has surprised you most over the years
- What is the issue or piece you are most proud of

### Vision and frustration
- If you came back full-time tomorrow, what would you build first
- What stopped you from raising prices in the last 5 years
- What did you try with the social media role that did not work, and what would you try differently
- What would you wish you had done 5 years ago that you did not

### Customers
- Of your top 5 advertisers, how long have they been with you and what do they pay
- Who has left in the last 2-3 years and why
- How often do advertisers ask you for digital help
- Do customers measure ROI from the paper, or is it relationship and habit

### Operations
- Walk me through a typical month, day by day in publication week
- What breaks when something breaks
- What is your relationship with the Pennsylvania printer like, what do their costs look like, what happens if they raise rates
- Who else in the country is doing what you are doing, and how do you compare

### The 6 papers from late 2024
- Walk me through each one. How long to break even? Which exceeded expectations? Which underperformed?

### The hard one (save for later in conversation, after rapport)
- The three prior deals that did not close. From your perspective, what was missing in those buyers? What are you looking for that you did not get?

### Specific items
- njlocalinfo.com - is that a Renna asset, and is it part of the deal?
- Cranford Monthly - I notice no 2026 issues are posted on the site yet, is that paused or just a posting lag?
- Peterstown and Pride of North Plainfield - the website lists them but I do not see recent issues, are those still active?

## What NOT to Talk About

- **Price.** Do not name a number. If asked, say you are still modeling and want to understand the business better before committing to a structure.
- **Customer concentration math** ($75K from one customer kills the SDE)
- **Your normalized SDE estimate** ($130-140K, not $179K)
- **Your target offer range** ($375-425K)
- **Your integration model** (Excel file with the $400K base case)
- **Eric's off-sell-side concern** (post-close cash crunch). Do not bring up that Eric said this; it puts Eric in a bad spot.
- **The mailing universe analysis** (Census reconciliation work) - keep that powder dry for diligence
- **The page-count analysis** - same

If Joe asks about price, redirect to structure. "I am thinking about how to make this work for both of us. The structure matters more to me than hitting a specific headline number."

## Stock vs Asset Sale

Eric raised this. If Joe brings it up:
- Stay open. "I want to understand the trade-offs."
- Do not commit either direction. The decision depends on what is in the entity (debt, contracts, lawsuits, tax position).
- Frame: "If the entity is clean, stock sale could be cleaner for the USPS and printer relationships, and gives me the credit history."

## The Seller Note

Joe is offering 50% seller financing at 8.0% over 36 months. This is generous on size, aggressive on rate and term. Do not negotiate at the lunch. File for LOI.

Levers to consider for the LOI:
- Lower rate (target 6-7%)
- Longer term (60 months instead of 36) to ease cash flow
- Interest-only year 1 to fund growth investment
- Performance-based earnout component (% of revenue growth shared back to seller)
- Standby provisions tied to advertiser retention

## Read-the-Room Cues

Watch for:
- Does Joe describe the business as growing, flat, or declining? His language matters more than his numbers.
- Does he get specific or vague when asked about customers and economics?
- Does he show curiosity about you, or just sell?
- Does he mention the prior buyers or avoid them?
- How does he respond to AI / digital / modernization talk - excited, defensive, or dismissive?
