# CIM Summary - Renna Media LLC

**Source:** `inputs/renna-media-cim.pdf` (18 pages, dated Jan 2026)
**Broker:** Murphy Business Sales - Westfield, Eric Sharret

---

## Business

- **Company:** Renna Media LLC, 202 Walnut Avenue, Cranford NJ 07016
- **Entity:** S-Corp, incorporated June 2000; Renna Media branded name dates to 2008 (first paper: Kenilworth)
- **Predecessor:** Rentec Design Studio (founded 1984) - production studio for publishers, ad agencies, printers; evolved into ad agency, then publisher
- **NAICS:** 513110 (Newspaper Publishers); SIC: 2711
- **Ownership:** Joe Renna 50%, Tina Renna 50%

## Product

- 30 hyper-local town newspapers
- Mailed to every home and business in each town (total saturation)
- 206,000 newspapers direct mailed monthly across Essex, Morris, Union, Somerset counties NJ
- Distribution via USPS
- Ad sizes: business card up to full page; price break every three towns
- In-house design and layout services included
- Also prints color flyers and menus inserted with newspapers (priced at 25% of standalone postcard cost)

## Customers

- 120 active advertisers monthly
- Top 15% of advertisers = 60% of revenue
- 80% recurring
- 10% seasonal repeat (landscaping, home services)
- 10% news (one-off) advertisers
- Pricing raised once since 2008 (in 2021 due to COVID-related production cost increases)

## Sales / Marketing

- All sales currently inbound, characterized as customer service
- No outside sales or marketing in recent years
- Social media manager position eliminated in 2024
- "Rudimentary" online presence per CIM

## Operations

- No physical office (home-based)
- 2 owners + previously 1 part-time staff (eliminated 2024)
- Printing and distribution outsourced to third-party vendors
- Owner time: ~160 hrs/month combined, concentrated in week before publication
- Joe: art director, layout, graphics, bookkeeping, sales, customer service
- Tina: editor-in-chief, content sourcing, editorial oversight, invoicing
- CIM claim: combined responsibilities could be transitioned to a single full-time employee

## Deal Terms

- **Asking price:** $500,000
- **Down payment:** $250,000
- **Seller financing:** $250,000 at 8.0% over 36 months ($7,834/mo)
- **Seller note security:** Behind primary lender, personal guaranty of buyer, periodic financials, life insurance on buyer favoring seller (depending on amount)
- **Transition:** 4 weeks free, additional via consulting agreement
- **Non-compete:** 5 years, 25 miles
- **Reason for sale:** Retirement

### Notable Line Item Movements

- **Salaries and wages:** $52K (2022) -> $56K (2023) -> $48K (2024) -> $5K (2025). Massive cut.
- **Employee benefits:** $0 (2022) -> $33K (2023) -> $34K (2024) -> $10K (2025). Cut alongside salary.
- **Officer compensation:** $192K (2022) -> $174K (2023) -> $60K (2024) -> $72K (2025). Owners cut their own pay materially.
- **COGS jumped to 78% in 2024** - tied to launching 6 new papers; partial recovery in 2025 to 76%.

### Implications (already noted in analysis)

The 2025 SDE rebound is roughly $50K of headcount savings vs 2023 baseline. A buyer who restores the social media role and adds management capacity will see normalized SDE in the $130-150K band, not $179K.

## Growth Pitch (Per CIM)

- Add ~12 more towns -> potentially triple revenue (NJ has 566 municipalities)
- Add 4 employees including a full-time manager -> potentially double revenue with incremental costs covered by added income
- Expand into full-service ad agency offerings (digital, promotional, graphic design, PR)
- Add EDDM services (Every Door Direct Mail)
- Regional editorial supplements (sports, real estate)
- Rebuild social media network (was monetized previously)
