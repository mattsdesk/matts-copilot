## Spreadsheet Notation Clarified

- "IN" = color insert; quantity in column D, towns listed in the cell
- "PR" suffix (e.g. "4+ PR") = press release accompanying the ad, run as filler if space allows
- "X" next to an advertiser = filler ad on standby (e.g. Cranford Barber Shop, Old Motorcycles)
- Page number notation (e.g. "Page 3") = advertiser paid $100 extra for placement
  - First right of refusal applies: Garden Communities holds Page 3 priority over Gabrielle Fitness
  - "BC" = back cover premium placement
- "MG" = make good, a free extra town given to compensate for a missed ad the prior month
- "EX" = extra free placement, often given to nonprofits in their home town as added value

## Revenue Model and Profitability

- Accrual-based revenue accounting being reverse-engineered from the spreadsheet
- Unit-based pricing varies too wildly to divide by unit; approach is to split monthly revenue (e.g. $4,300) across the 30 papers proportionally
- Per-paper profitability varies sharply: small towns (e.g. Garwood at $667 cost) are highly profitable; large towns (e.g. Linden at ~$5,000) are near break-even or negative
  - Regional advertisers are what make the whole network profitable: "they lift all the boats"
- New clusters of six towns scale well even without active marketing (Millburn example)
- Montclair area flagged as a natural next cluster: Cedar Grove, Glen Ridge, Montclair, Verona
