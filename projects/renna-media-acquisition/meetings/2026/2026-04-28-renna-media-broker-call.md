## Business Overview

- Renna Media: 40-year-old community newspaper business
- Monthly publications mailed directly to homes (not left at stores)
- 30 towns currently covered in New Jersey
- Founded by Joe and Tina (husband/wife team)
- Joe: 65, turning 66 this month, media/advertising background
- Started official newspaper operations in 2008
- Revenue model: $1M annual revenue, ~$150K SDE
- Inbound advertising only, no outbound sales
- Content sourced from local organizations (Lions Club, charities, events)
- Tina rewrites press releases, Joe layouts using legacy software
- Expansion strategy: adds 6 towns at a time (Joe's "magic number")

## Operational Details

- Production process: 4 days/month creating newspapers
- Uses outdated software (original Apple computer system, not Adobe)
- Printing outsourced to Pennsylvania facility
- Switched printers couple years ago (Connecticut one shut down)
- Pricing: hasn't raised prices since COVID (only increase since 2008)
- Paper specs: typically 16 pages (8 content, 8 ads)
- Could expand to 20 pages with minimal cost increase
- High advertiser retention rates
- Postage costs rising 7% annually

## Financial Structure and Risks

- Asking price: $250K with 50% seller note (note: per project memory, full asking is $500K with $250K down and $250K seller note)
- High COGS, low margins (~20% gross margin)
- Customer concentration risk with top advertisers
- Fixed costs concerns: postage rate increases, printing cost stability, relationship transferability with vendors
- Growth requires additional investment for staff hiring (graphic designer, salesperson), system modernization, expansion capital

## Growth Opportunities

- Geographic expansion: 500+ towns available in New Jersey
- Technology modernization: AI content processing, Adobe conversion
- Pricing optimization: significant room for rate increases
- Digital assets: nj.localinfo.com (ownership to be confirmed)
