## Effective discount at 6-town tier vs single-town stack

Comparing 6-town tier price to running the same ad in 6 separate single-town placements.

|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Unit | $600 | $360 | 40% |
| 2 Units | $900 | $560 | 38% |
| 4 Units | $1,440 | $1,100 | 24% |
| 6 Units | $1,920 | $1,200 | 38% |
| 9 Units (Half Page) | $2,160 | $1,300 | 40% |
| 12 Units | $2,400 | $1,400 | 42% |
| 18 Units (Full Page) | $3,000 | $1,800 | 40% |

The "38% at 6 towns" framing is roughly right, with 4-Unit being the only outlier (24%). The compression intensifies further at 12-30 town tiers and is even more aggressive at the full-page sizes.

## Implications for diligence

- Rate card discounting is **intentionally steep** at 6 towns. The fact that it's published right in the paper means advertisers know it. The book is trained on the discount.
- The "mostly recurring" 80%+ retention claim is consistent with a discount-anchored book where the deal is the primary stickiness.
- True margin recovery levers post-close are limited to: (1) front-page premium inventory, (2) back-page premium, (3) full-color upsell (paper is currently black-on-white per rate sheet), (4) digital cross-sell via Montclair Digital.
- Compressing the discount curve in year 1 will churn the multi-town saturation accounts which is where the volume sits. Time it for year 2+ with grandfathering.
