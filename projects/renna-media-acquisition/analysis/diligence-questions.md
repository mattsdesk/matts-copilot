# Diligence Questions - Renna Media

**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Audience:** Eric Sharret (broker), Joe and Tina Renna (sellers, post-NDA refresh)
**Format:** Send broker as written list. Owner call to verify and probe specifics.

---

## Top 3 Critical Unknowns

If anything else gets dropped, do not drop these.

1. **Retention driver:** relationship vs ROI vs habit. Determines if revenue survives transition.
2. **Advertiser ROI:** do customers see measurable results. Determines long-term durability.
3. **Operational dependency:** end-to-end monthly cycle, what is documented vs in heads. Determines whether you can run it day one.

---

## 1. Customer Retention - Real or Fragile?

**What to learn:** churn patterns, tenure, why customers leave when they leave.

**Asks:**
- Advertiser retention cohort data by month or year for the past 3 years
- Tenure distribution: how many advertisers have been with Renna 3+ years, 5+ years, 10+ years
- Churn rate: % of revenue lost to churn in 2023, 2024, 2025
- Reasons given when advertisers stop (close down, switch channel, dissatisfied, other)
- Of the top 20 advertisers, how many have a personal relationship with Joe or Tina vs purely transactional

## 2. Advertiser ROI

**What to learn:** does this actually work for the people paying for it.

**Asks:**
- How do advertisers measure success today (call tracking, coupons, anecdotal)
- Testimonials, case studies, or unsolicited feedback from current advertisers
- Which industries perform best by retention and reorder rate
- Which industries churn fastest
- Any observed change in advertiser response rates over the past 3-5 years

## 3. Revenue Concentration

**What to learn:** how fragile the top tier is.

**Asks:**
- Top 20 advertisers: name, vertical, monthly spend, tenure, towns advertised in
- Of the top 20, how many spend $10K+ annually
- Have any large advertisers left in the past 2-3 years
- Revenue mix by vertical (home services vs retail vs professional services vs other)
- Largest single customer concentration as % of total revenue

## 4. Transition Risk - End-to-End Operations

**What to learn:** the actual monthly rhythm and where it can break.

**Asks:**
- Walk me through a full monthly publication cycle, day by day
- Most stressful weeks of the cycle and why
- Where mistakes happen today and how they are caught
- What would break in month 1 if Joe and Tina were not available
- What processes are documented vs lived in your head
- Tools used: layout software, accounting, CRM, email, USPS portal, anything else

## 5. Vendor Dependence

**What to learn:** stability of the print and mailing supply chain.

**Asks:**
- Names of printing and mailing vendors
- Are there contracts, MSAs, or just handshake relationships
- Postage line broken out of COGS for 2022-2025
- Print cost per unit history over the past 3 years
- What would per-unit cost look like if monthly volume dropped 20%
- What backup printers or mailers exist if the primary fails
- Is the USPS account in the entity's name and assignable to a buyer

## 6. Sales Engine - Demand vs Passive Sufficiency

**What to learn:** how much real demand exists vs how much they harvest passively.

**Asks:**
- Monthly inbound inquiry volume from prospective advertisers
- Inquiry to paying advertiser conversion rate
- Has outbound sales ever been tried and what happened
- Industries or geographies considered untapped
- If you wanted to double advertiser count in 12 months, what would you do

## 7. Pricing Power

**What to learn:** can the buyer raise prices without bleeding the book.

**Asks:**
- What happened in 2021 when prices were raised: any churn, any pushback
- How often do advertisers negotiate pricing, and how often do you discount
- % of customers on legacy pricing vs current rate card
- Any advertisers on multi-year price locks
- How do current rates compare to alternatives (other shoppers, EDDM direct, Patch, NextDoor ads)

## 8. Expansion Model

**What to learn:** is the "scalable launch" claim real.

**Asks:**
- Performance of the 6 papers launched in 2024: revenue, advertiser count, breakeven status by month
- Time to profitability for the most recent 6 launches
- % of advertisers in new towns who were already advertising in adjacent Renna papers vs net-new
- Have any towns failed or underperformed expectations
- Breakeven advertiser count per town
- Cost to launch a single new town (capex + first 6 months opex)

## 9. Digital Opportunity

**What to learn:** is the cross-sell to a Montclair Digital book real or fantasy.

**Asks:**
- Have advertisers asked for digital services and how often
- Has any digital service offering ever been tested
- Of current top 20 advertisers, do you know which have working websites, run Google Ads, or have an SEO presence
- Would your customers trust Renna to manage Google Ads or SEO on their behalf
- Do any local competitors offer print + digital bundled services

## 10. Owner Motivation

**What to learn:** are they truly retiring or already coasting.

**Asks:**
- Hours worked per week 3-5 years ago vs today
- What have you intentionally stopped doing (services, outreach, events)
- If you worked full-time again, what would you focus on
- Do you believe the business is growing, flat, or declining
- Why now for the sale (specific trigger, not just "retirement")

---

## Financial Documentation Asks

Beyond the questions above, request before LOI:

- Tax returns 2022-2025 (all schedules, complete)
- Accrual P&L for same period (CIM is cash basis tax returns)
- AR aging reports for last 12 months
- AP aging reports for last 12 months
- Bank statements for last 12 months
- Detailed COGS breakdown (print, postage, design tools, other)
- Detailed officer compensation, employee benefits, and personal expense add-backs with receipts or schedules
- Advertiser invoice file (anonymized OK) showing monthly billing detail
- USPS shipping/postage statements for 2024 and 2025
- Vendor contracts (printing, mailing, software, leases)
- Any pending or recent legal claims, customer disputes, or regulatory issues

---

## Owner Call Format (When Scheduled)

90 minutes. Walk the monthly cycle first. Then concentration. Then digital demand. Save retention drivers for last so the conversation has built rapport.

Bring a notebook. Do not lead the witness. Listen for what they choose to emphasize and what they avoid.
