# Investor Analysis - Renna Media

**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Status:** Initial

---

## Headline Take

Asset is interesting only if you intend to actively integrate it with Montclair Digital. As a passive cash-flow play it is mediocre and overpriced. As a customer acquisition channel into ~120 NJ SMBs plus a monthly distribution moat into 206K homes, it is asymmetric.

**Probability-weighted recommendation:** Pursue with structured diligence and a $375K-$425K offer with retention-based earnout.

## What You're Actually Buying

| Layer | Description |
|---|---|
| Cash-flow asset | $1M revenue print business, ~$140K normalized SDE, low-growth, USPS-exposed |
| Customer list | ~120 active SMB advertisers in core MD geographic radius |
| Distribution channel | Monthly delivery into 206K NJ homes/businesses across 30 towns |
| Brand | Trusted local presence in 30 NJ municipalities with 17+ years operating history |
| Operations | Production rhythm, vendor relationships, USPS account |

The standalone print business pays for the SBA debt service and keeps the lights on. The customer list and distribution channel are the actual prize.

## Where the ChatGPT Memo Is Wrong or Soft

**1. There IS customer concentration.** Memo says "no customer concentration." CIM says top 15% = 60% of revenue. That is roughly 18 accounts. Loss of 5 of those 18 = 15-20% revenue hit. This is the single biggest underwriting risk and the memo dismisses it.

**2. The 2025 SDE rebound is partly artificial.** Salaries went $48K (2024) to $5K (2025). Employee benefits went $34K to $10K. They eliminated the social media role and cut hours. A buyer who actually runs the business needs to add headcount back. Normalized 2025 SDE is closer to $130-140K, in line with the 3-year average. Use $140K, not $179K, when underwriting.

**3. The "scalable launch" claim is not supported by the numbers.** They launched 6 new papers in 2024. Revenue dropped that year (to $948K). 2025 only recovered to roughly 2022 levels. Where is the operating leverage? COGS is 76-78% and stuck there. Adding a town adds print and postage. USPS does not give volume discounts that meaningfully change unit economics.

**4. Pricing power is weaker than the CIM frames it.** They raised prices once since 2008. That is not "untapped pricing." That is a relationship-driven seller who has not tested elasticity in 17 years and has 90% recurring advertisers who would notice a hike.

**5. Valuation is at the top of the SBA range.** $500K / $179K = 2.8x recent SDE. $500K / $140K (3-yr avg) = 3.6x. SBA Main Street deals typically trade 2.0-3.5x SDE. Print/declining-industry assets should sit at the low end. Real defensible price is $375K-$425K.

## Where the ChatGPT Memo Is Right

- The "stable cash-flow asset vs platform to transform" framing is correct.
- The Operational Upgrade case (sales engine + pricing + CRM) is the cleanest upside scenario.
- The Platform Transformation case (full local marketing platform) is the real prize but requires rebuild while running.
- Hidden risk: everything lives in two heads. Real and underweighted.
- Strategic fit question is the right question to ask.

## Montclair Digital Integration Thesis

**Premise:** Renna Media's 120 advertisers are pre-qualified prospects for MD's services because they have already demonstrated:

- Active local marketing budget
- Willingness to pay monthly recurring fees
- Trust in the Renna brand and channel

**Conversion math (illustrative, conservative):**

| Conversion rate | Advertisers converted | Avg MD ARPU/yr | Annual MD revenue add |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5% | 6 | $12,000 | $72,000 |
| 10% | 12 | $12,000 | $144,000 |
| 15% | 18 | $12,000 | $216,000 |
| 20% | 24 | $12,000 | $288,000 |

Even 5% conversion adds $72K/yr to MD at high gross margin. That alone services the seller note. 10% conversion makes the deal genuinely attractive. 20% is the upside case and should be tested with a pilot list of 10-20 known accounts before close.

**Open question for diligence:** Have advertisers ever asked Joe and Tina for digital services? How often? Per CIM, "management is frequently asked to provide" agency services. Quantify this.

## Risks (Underwriting Priority Order)

1. **Customer concentration + relationship-driven retention.** Top 15% = 60% of revenue. If those relationships don't transfer, the model breaks.
2. **Operational dependency.** Two-person business with undocumented monthly cycle. 4-week transition is too short.
3. **Margin compression from USPS / print costs.** COGS already 76-78%. One USPS rate hike crushes the model.
4. **Owner motivation / coast effect.** They cut their own work hours significantly. Are the 2024-2025 numbers a true picture or are they riding inertia?
5. **Print decline.** Real but not catastrophic for hyper-local where there is no digital substitute with comparable saturation reach.
6. **Cross-sell fantasy risk.** MD conversion thesis is unproven. Could be lower than 5%.

## Recommended Deal Structure

- **Purchase price:** $400K
- **Cash at close:** $200K (40% of asking is normal opening; 50% if seller pushes back)
- **Seller note:** $150K at 7% over 36 months
- **Earnout:** $50K paid Year 1 contingent on retention of 85% of top 20 advertisers measured at month 12
- **Transition:** 90 days minimum (vs 4 weeks offered), with month-by-month consulting agreement extending to 6 months
- **Non-compete:** Accept 5yr/25mi as offered

The earnout neutralizes the customer concentration risk. The 90-day transition is non-negotiable given the operational dependency. The price reflects real normalized SDE, not the 2025 SDE-after-headcount-cuts number.
