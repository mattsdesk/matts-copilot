**Date:** 2026-04-15
**Status:** Initial assessment

## Executive Take

Solid, cash-flowing, operationally simple business with real upside. Not a no-brainer at asking price without confirming key risks.

## What's Good

**1. Strong, consistent demand**
- Fully booked; road tests booked ~1 year out
- Required service, not discretionary
- Regulatory tailwind: NJ 50-hour supervised driving requirement (Feb 2025, enforcement ramping 2026)

**2. Simple model**
- Three drivers: instructors, cars, scheduling
- No complex inventory, tech, or supply chain

**3. Semi-absentee potential**
- Owner at ~3–4 hrs/day
- Staff handles operations
- Matters if goal is portfolio of businesses, not a job

**4. Clean growth lever**
- Add cars + instructors = add revenue
- Not theoretical -- mechanical

---

## Risks

**1. People business**
- Instructors are everything ($22–$32/hr)
- Risks: hiring pipeline, retention, quality control
- If instructor scaling fails, growth stalls immediately

**2. "Fully booked" could be misleading**
- High demand OR operational bottleneck
- Must confirm: are they turning away business, or poorly scheduled?

**3. Customer acquisition is unclear**
- CIM mentions DMV relationships, schools, referrals
- No detail on paid marketing, digital presence, or lead flow
- This is where Matt's skillset could add significant value

**4. Asset intensity**
- Vehicles: $80–90K + ongoing gas (large, volatile line), maintenance, insurance
- Not a high-margin, software-like business
- Margins compress as you scale

**5. Real estate decision**
- Delta: $600K for the property
- Unit 2 vacant; Unit 3 lease expired Jan 2026
- Marlboro DS lease only through Mar 2027
- Must evaluate independently: is it a good commercial RE investment, or an overpriced mediocre asset?

## Pre-LOI Checklist

Before any offer:

**1. Validate demand vs bottleneck**
- How many leads/month?
- How many turned away?

**2. Validate instructor pipeline**
- How long to hire and certify?
- Historical attrition rate?

**3. Validate scheduling utilization**
- % of instructor time booked
- Idle time vs unmet demand

**4. Regulatory / compliance (from deep research — critical)**
- Who is the Qualified Supervising Instructor (QSI)? Is it the owner?
  - QSI requires: licensed instructor 2+ years, 500+ BTW hrs, 3-credit NJ driver ed college course
  - If owner is the QSI and leaves, license cannot be renewed — existential risk
- Get deferred revenue schedule: road tests booked ~1 yr out = significant prepaid liabilities owed to students
- Confirm all instructor licenses are current and renewals filed 30 days prior
- Confirm each vehicle is on the insurance certificate with compliant limits ($250K/$500K/$50K or $500K CSL)
- Confirm dual controls documented on each vehicle
- Confirm Manalapan CCO is current and assignable to new owner
- Confirm Marlboro DS lease (Mar 2027 expiry) is assignable; get landlord consent requirements
- Confirm 3 years of student records are on site and audit-ready
- Confirm no pending NJMVC enforcement actions or open complaints

## Negotiation Anchor

- Target price: $500K–$575K (business only)
- Levers: people dependency, asset intensity, regulatory risk, deferred revenue obligations, growth not yet realized
- If QSI issue is real (owner is sole qualifier), price down further or require owner transition period of 12+ months

---

## Regulatory Quick Reference

- NJMVC school license requires $10K surety bond + QSI on staff at all times
- Instructor hiring is not fast: age/license/background check/DMV written + driving test
- Max 3 hrs BTW instruction per student per day — affects scheduling and revenue per car
- Student records must be retained 3 years at principal location; service agreements mandatory; 72-hr rescission right after first lesson
- Instructors are W-2 (confirmed by CIM); NJ ABC test makes IC classification high-risk
- Manalapan CCO and zoning approval required for any location change
