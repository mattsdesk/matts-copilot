## Renna Article Output
**ESSEX COUNTY OPENS DOORS FOR RESPONSIBLE ELECTRONICS DISPOSAL**

On May 16, residents will have the opportunity to responsibly retire their outdated electronics at no cost.

Essex County's Public Works facility in Cedar Grove will host its Computer and Electronics Recycling Day from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. The event provides a convenient, environmentally conscious way for residents to dispose of technology they no longer need.

The recycling program accepts a broad range of electronic equipment, from desktop and laptop computers to mobile devices, televisions, and home entertainment systems. Printers, scanners, fax machines, and office equipment are welcome, as are household electronics like toasters, irons, and stereos. The county will also collect outdated telecom gear, VCRs, DVD players, and related devices.

Not all electronics qualify: smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarms will not be accepted due to safety regulations surrounding hazardous materials contained within these devices.

The program is exclusively for Essex County residents. To participate, bring your electronics to the Public Works building at 99 West Bradford Avenue in Cedar Grove. Residents must show proof of residency at check-in. For those with commercial quantities of electronic waste, this event is not available—the program is designed for household disposal only.

According to county officials, the initiative reflects growing concern about environmental stewardship. Improperly discarded electronics release lead, mercury, and other toxic substances into the waste stream. By centralizing collection, the county prevents contamination of soil and water while ensuring materials are processed safely.

For questions about what qualifies or details about timing, call the Essex County Utilities Authority at 973-792-9060. Additional information is available at ecuanj.com.

The event is jointly sponsored by Essex County and the Essex County Utilities Authority.
